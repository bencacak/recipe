Cincinnati Chili

A Greek style pasta sauce served over spagetti topped with medium cheddar cheese and chopped onion. May be also served on top of a hotdog or as standalone chili.

Recipe Information

  • Prep Time: 10 Minutes
  • Cook Time: 60 Minutes
  • Serves: 6 to 8

Ingredients

  • 1 Pound Lean Ground Meat
  • 4 Ounces Tomato Paste
  • 5 Cups Water
  • 1 Packet Gold Star Cincinnati Chili Seasoning
  • 1 Box Spagetti
  • 1 Medium Onion
  • 1 Bag Shredded Mild Cheddar Cheese
  • 1 Fluid Ounce Frank's Red Hot Sauce

Directions

  • 1. In a large pot, combine your ground meat, water, and tomato paste. Mash the ingredients with fork until the meat is at an even consistency and the tomato paste is thoroughly mixed in.
  • 2. Add the contents of the seasoning packet into the pot and set to high heat. Put the lid on and let it boil covered for three minutes.
  • 3. While you bring the contents of the pot to a boil, chop half of an onion. Add the onion and your hot sauce into the pot.
  • 4. Once the chili has boiled for three minutes, lower the heat slightly and let the chili simmer for 40 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  • 5. When the chili has appoximately 20 minutes left of cook time, start the process of cooking the spagetti. Follow the directions on the box. Chop the rest of the onion.
  • 6. The spagetti should finish cooking when the chili is done. Turn off the heat to the chili and strain the spagetti.
  • 7. Place the spagetti into a bowl and scoop some chili on top. Be sure to add the raw, chopped onion, hot sauce, and cheese as desired.
